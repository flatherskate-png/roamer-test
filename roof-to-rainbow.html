East Africa to the Cape — this is the route that redraws your sense of what the world contains. Victoria Falls alone drops more water per second than almost any falls on earth, and will make every waterfall you see for the rest of your life feel like a consolation prize. Budget six to eight weeks if you're going overland, which is the only way to really feel the distance. This is not a trip you take. It's a trip that takes you.

The Route

Six stops.
One continent, edge to edge.

01
Stop 01 · Kenya

Masai Mara, Kenya

The savanna at dawn, before the heat arrives and the world goes still.

The Masai Mara is where the word "safari" earns its meaning. Across 1,500 square kilometres of open grassland, the density of large mammals is among the highest on earth — lions, elephants, cheetahs, giraffes, and in July through October, the Great Migration: two million wildebeest and zebra crossing the Mara River in a spectacle of noise, dust, and crocodiles. Hot air balloons rise at sunrise. The light at that hour, on that landscape, is unreasonable.

02
Stop 02 · Tanzania

Mount Kilimanjaro, Tanzania

The highest point in Africa rises from flat savanna with no warning and no apology.

Kilimanjaro is the world's tallest free-standing mountain — 5,895 metres of volcanic rock that rises improbably from the Tanzanian plain. You don't need to summit to be awed by it; the approach through cloud forest and moorland is extraordinary in itself. Those who do attempt the summit take 6–8 days via the Marangu or Lemosho routes. The town of Moshi at its base is relaxed and well-equipped for climbers and non-climbers alike.

Side Quests Along the Way
🌿
Arusha National Park
Flamingos, giraffes, and Meru mountain in the shadow of Kilimanjaro. A half-day from the airport.
🦁
Serengeti & Ngorongoro
If you have time before flying east: the Serengeti and Ngorongoro Crater are northwest of Kilimanjaro and among the world's great wildlife destinations. A significant detour but rarely regretted.
Serengeti and Ngorongoro are northwest of Kilimanjaro, not on the route east to Zanzibar — flagged as a deliberate detour, not a pass-through.
03
Stop 03 · Tanzania

Zanzibar, Tanzania

An island that has been a spice market, a sultanate, a trading post, and a paradise, often simultaneously.

Zanzibar's Stone Town is a UNESCO World Heritage labyrinth of carved doors, narrow alleys, mosques, and markets that smells permanently of cloves and cardamom. The island's east coast beaches — Nungwi, Kendwa, Paje — are legitimately among the finest in the Indian Ocean: white sand, turquoise water, dhows on the horizon. This is where you slow down after the Mara and Kilimanjaro. Give it at least four days.

Side Quests Along the Way
🦛
South Luangwa, Zambia
One of Africa's finest walking safari destinations. Legitimate detour if flying into Zambia — roughly en route to Victoria Falls from the north.
🐊
Lower Zambezi National Park
Canoe safaris on the Zambezi with hippos and elephants on the banks. Remote and extraordinary.
This is the longest leg and almost everyone flies it. The overland route via Zambia is possible but involves multiple border crossings and 3–4 hard days. South Luangwa is northwest of Lusaka — verify it's actually en route to Victoria Falls before presenting it as a connector stop.
04
Stop 04 · Zambia / Zimbabwe

Victoria Falls, Zambia / Zimbabwe

Mosi-oa-Tunya. The smoke that thunders. The locals named it first and named it correctly.

Victoria Falls is the largest waterfall on earth by the measure that matters most — the sheer volume of water going over the edge. At peak flow the mist rises 400 metres and can be seen from 50 kilometres away. The falls straddle the Zambia–Zimbabwe border; both sides offer different vantage points and both are worth crossing for. The Devil's Pool, a natural infinity pool at the lip of the falls on the Zambian side, is either thrilling or insane depending on your disposition. Possibly both.

Side Quests Along the Way
🦁
Chobe National Park, Botswana
The highest concentration of elephants in Africa. Directly accessible from Victoria Falls by road — a natural add-on before heading south.
🌿
Okavango Delta, Botswana
The world's largest inland delta — water flowing into the Kalahari sand. Mokoro canoe safaris through papyrus channels. A significant detour west but one of Africa's singular experiences.
🏜️
Etosha National Park, Namibia
Night drives around a vast salt pan where predators and prey converge at lit waterholes. En route from Windhoek toward the Namib.
Chobe is genuinely adjacent to Victoria Falls and a low-friction add-on. Okavango is a significant detour west — worth flagging as a deliberate diversion. Etosha's position relative to the Windhoek–Namib route needs verification before presenting as "along the way."
05
Stop 05 · Namibia

Namib Desert, Namibia

The oldest desert on earth. It has been practicing silence for 55 million years.

The Namib is the world's oldest desert and among its most visually extraordinary — rust-red dunes cresting at 300 metres, dead acacia trees in the white clay pan of Deadvlei, fog rolling in from the Atlantic each morning to feed a desert ecosystem that exists nowhere else. Sossusvlei and Deadvlei are the iconic destinations; Dune 45 is the one you climb at sunrise, thighs burning, to watch the shadows pull long across the sand below. The light here is a different quality than anywhere else.

Age: ~55 million years oldDune 45: ~170 m tallDeadvlei trees: 900 yrs deadBest: May–SepDon't miss: Deadvlei at sunrise

Side Quests Along the Way
🌸
Namaqualand (Aug–Sep)
In spring, this otherwise arid stretch between Namibia and the Cape explodes with wildflowers — one of the most surprising spectacles in southern Africa.
🦭
Cape Cross Seal Colony
~100,000 Cape fur seals on a remote beach north of Swakopmund. Noisy, smelly, unforgettable.
🌊

A surreal German colonial town on the Namibian Atlantic coast — bakeries, sand dunes, and quad bikes. Good overnight before the long drive south.
Cape Cross is north of Swakopmund on the coastal road — if driving the N7 inland route, it requires a detour. Worth flagging whether the coastal or inland route makes more sense for this leg.
06
Stop 06 · Journey's End

Cape Town, South Africa

A mountain inside a city, two oceans meeting at the point below, and the best light in the southern hemisphere.

Cape Town ends the route on its own terms — dramatic, beautiful, and slightly unreal after weeks of wilderness. Table Mountain is the first thing and the last thing; take the cable car up at sunset and watch the city go golden below. The Cape Peninsula drive south to Cape Point passes through boulder-strewn penguin colonies, cliff-edge coastal roads, and the meeting of the Atlantic and Indian Oceans. The Winelands are an hour east. The food scene is serious. You will not be ready to leave.

Before you go.

Overland or Fly

The honest answer is both. The Masai Mara–Kilimanjaro–Zanzibar triangle is manageable overland. Zanzibar to Victoria Falls is a fly leg for most travelers. Victoria Falls to the Namib can be overland via Botswana if you have the time and will. Namib to Cape Town is the one leg that rewards driving above all else.

When to Go

June through October is the sweet spot for the wildlife portions — dry season means animals concentrate around water and vegetation is low. Zanzibar is good year-round with two dry seasons. The Namaqualand wildflower bloom is August–September only.

Visas

Kenya, Tanzania, Zambia, Zimbabwe, Namibia, and South Africa each have their own requirements. Most nationalities can get East African e-visas covering Kenya and Tanzania together. A KAZA UniVisa covers Zambia and Zimbabwe and is available at major border crossings. Check requirements early.

How Long

Six weeks is the minimum to do this without feeling rushed. Eight is comfortable. The temptation to linger — particularly in Zanzibar and the Namib — is real and should be budgeted for. This is not a route that rewards being efficient about it.

Health

Malaria prophylaxis is required for Kenya, Tanzania, Zambia, Zimbabwe, and northern Namibia. Yellow fever vaccination may be required depending on your route and nationality. Get travel vaccinations 4–6 weeks before departure. Cape Town is malaria-free.

The Scale Question

Africa is larger than you think — larger than the US, China, India, and most of Europe combined. The distances on this route are real. What looks manageable on a map is often two hard days of driving. Build in buffer days. The continent will use them.
