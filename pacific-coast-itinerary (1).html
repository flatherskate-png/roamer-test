This is the drive people mean when they say "road trip." From the emerald archipelago of Puget Sound to the sun-bleached sprawl of Los Angeles, the Pacific Coast traces the edge of a continent — one slow, spectacular mile at a time. You don't rush this. You couldn't if you tried.

The Route

Six stops.
One unforgettable coast.

01
Stop 01 · Departure Point

Seattle, WA

Where cloud cover is a lifestyle choice and the coffee is non-negotiable.

Your journey begins in a city that has never apologized for its drizzle. Seattle sits at the intersection of mountains, sea, and software money — a place where a waterfront fish market and a world-class art scene coexist without irony. Spend a night or three before you point the car south. You'll want to have the Space Needle in your rearview mirror, not your windshield.

Elevation: 520 ft Pike Place Market est. 1907 Population 750,000+ Best: May–Sep Don't miss: Ferry to Bainbridge
3 hr 20 min south 175 mi · US-101
Side Quests Along the Way
🌊
Olympic National Park
Detour west for temperate rainforest and the Hoh River Trail. Budget a full day.
🚢
Astoria, OR
The oldest American settlement west of the Rockies. The column has 164 stairs and panoramic views.
🧀
Tillamook Creamery
Free samples and a factory tour. The ice cream line is worth the wait.
02
Stop 02 · Oregon Coast

Cannon Beach, OR

Haystack Rock rises from the surf like a plot twist you didn't see coming.

Oregon's coast belongs to everyone — state law mandates it. Which means the beaches here are wild, public, and bracingly cold in all the right ways. Cannon Beach is a small town that understands its assignment: excellent galleries, a few very good restaurants, and one of the most photographed sea stacks on earth. Tidal pools at the base of Haystack Rock are a legitimate highlight for anyone willing to get their shoes wet.

Haystack Rock: 235 ft tall Population ~1,700 Water temp: 50–55°F year-round Best: Jun–Sep Don't miss: Ecola State Park
4 hr 30 min south 265 mi · US-101
Side Quests Along the Way
🌬️
Cape Perpetua Overlook
A 500-ft headland with views up and down the coast. Thor's Well blowhole is otherworldly at high tide.
🦭
Sea Lion Caves
The largest sea cave in America. Wild Steller sea lions, year-round.
🍺
Arcata, CA
Quirky college town with a Victorian plaza and strong craft beer scene. Good overnight before the park.
03
Stop 03 · Northern California

Redwood National & State Parks

The trees were here before Rome. They'll be here after everything.

The coastal redwoods are the tallest living things on the planet — some cresting 380 feet — and they create a cathedral silence that cities spend millions trying to replicate. Drive the Avenue of the Giants with the windows down. Walk the Fern Canyon trail, where 50-foot fern-draped walls close in around you like a secret. You will feel, briefly but genuinely, very small. That is the whole point.

Tallest tree: 380 ft Trees up to 2,000 yrs old UNESCO World Heritage Site 40 mi of coastline in park Don't miss: Fern Canyon
5 hr south 300 mi · US-101 & CA-1
Side Quests Along the Way
🌉
Bodega Bay
Hitchcock filmed The Birds here. Atmospheric cliffs, fresh dungeness crab, half the tourists of everywhere else.
🦅
Point Reyes National Seashore
Elephant seals, a working lighthouse, and trails that feel genuinely remote an hour from the city.
🍷
Sonoma Coast & Valley
A quieter alternative to Napa. Better views, smaller crowds, equally good wine.
04
Stop 04 · The City

San Francisco, CA

The fog rolls in every afternoon. Nobody who lives here can explain why they love it. Everyone does.

San Francisco is the most European city in America and the most American city in Europe — a dense, hilly, beautiful paradox that has been reinventing itself since the Gold Rush. Walk across the Golden Gate. Eat a burrito in the Mission. Take the ferry to Alcatraz because you might as well. Give yourself at least three days; you'll regret two.

Golden Gate: 1.7 mi span 47 hills within city limits Population 875,000 Avg high: 65°F in Aug Don't miss: Marin Headlands view
2 hr 45 min south 155 mi · CA-1 scenic or US-101
Side Quests Along the Way
🦋
Santa Cruz
Monarch butterfly sanctuary, a beloved boardwalk, and surf culture without the LA price tag.
🐘
Año Nuevo State Park
Elephant seal colony right off the highway. December–March is peak season; they're massive and unbothered by you.
🌲
Muir Woods
Old-growth redwoods 12 miles from the Golden Gate. Book timed entry tickets in advance — this one fills up.
05
Stop 05 · The Crown Jewel

Big Sur, CA

90 miles of coastline so dramatic it was declared a National Scenic Byway in 1996 — the government's polite way of admitting what everyone already knew.

There is no town. There are no shortcuts. Big Sur is 90 miles of Highway 1 threading between cliff and ocean at heights that will make your passengers grip the door handle. Stop at Bixby Creek Bridge and stare. Pull over at McWay Falls where an 80-foot waterfall drops directly onto a beach no one can reach. Camp at Pfeiffer Big Sur if you can get a site. This is the section people drive the whole trip to see.

Bixby Bridge: 260 ft above sea 90 mi of protected coast McWay Falls: 80 ft Road may close seasonally No gas for 60+ mi stretches
3 hr 30 min south 240 mi · CA-1 & US-101
Side Quests Along the Way
🏰
Hearst Castle
A newspaper magnate's 165-room hilltop palace. Absurd and magnificent in equal measure. Tours run daily.
🦦
Morro Bay
A 576-ft volcanic plug rises from the harbor. Sea otters float in the estuary. Good fish tacos on the embarcadero.
🌿
Santa Barbara
Spanish colonial architecture, a farmers' market, and wine country an hour inland. Worth a full overnight stop.
06
Stop 06 · Journey's End

Los Angeles, CA

You have earned the right to sit in traffic and not mind. Look at the palm trees. You made it.

Los Angeles is not one city but several, loosely connected by a freeway system and a shared belief in reinvention. Arrive via the Pacific Coast Highway from the north — it's the only civilized entrance — and let Malibu ease you in. The Getty, the Griffith Observatory, a taco in East LA, a sunset at Venice Beach. The city rewards those who come without a plan and penalizes everyone else equally.

Population 4 million+ 300+ days of sunshine 290 mi of beaches in SoCal Best arrival: PCH from north Don't miss: Griffith at sunset

Before you go.

Best Time to Drive

May through October is ideal. Summer brings crowds but guarantees weather. September and October are the insider pick — thinner traffic, lower prices, same coastline. Winter closures on CA-1 at Big Sur are common; always check Caltrans before you go.

The Car Question

Rent the convertible. You will tell yourself you don't need it. You do. A proper road trip machine with good speakers and no roof makes Big Sur the experience it deserves to be. If you won't convert, at least get something with a sunroof.

Pacing Yourself

Two weeks is the minimum to do this without regret. Three is comfortable. The mileage is never the problem — it's the stops you can't stop making. Budget an extra day between Redwoods and San Francisco; that Humboldt stretch tends to steal afternoons.

Where to Sleep

Book Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park campground the day reservations open — they vanish. For towns, independent motels along the 101 tend to be cheerfully eccentric and well-priced. In San Francisco, pick a neighborhood hotel over a chain.

Gas & Practicalities

Fill up before Big Sur. There are stretches of 50+ miles with no services, and the one gas station that exists charges accordingly. The America the Beautiful pass ($80/year) covers Redwoods entry and pays for itself quickly.

What to Leave Behind

Your timeline. The whole drive is approximately 1,650 miles, but the point is to stop constantly. The Roamer version covers the highlights; the real version rewards everyone who pulls over for no particular reason and ends up staying an hour.
